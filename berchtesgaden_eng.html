Day 3 - Kehlstein 1837 m

View trail on mapy.cz

In the morning we go to Obersalzberg and park our car again in the same free of charge place as last time. We bought a ticket for the bus, which will take us to the Eagle's Nest. Road is very winding. Notice that it is a ban on hiking this route. It is very narrow road and all buses have radio transmitters to avoid buses on the opposite direction. As soon as we get off the bus, we are waiting in a line where every visitor has to report his departure time. Buses are constantly overcrowded, so any further change is almost impossible. After that we continue through the tunnel straight to the famous lift. Again we wait a bit, guides are trying to pull as many people as possible. But the waiting definitely worth. Everything looks new and luxurious. After visiting the house we continue to the top of Kehlstein mountain and along the Hoher Göll trail. In the middle of the path we decided to return because some parts seemed to be risky without a ferrat equipment. On the way back we decided not to go by a lift (mainly because loads of people) but walk down the trail. We took the bus down and check the opening hours of the Documentation Museum and bus timetable (from Berchtesgaden) for next time. We wanted take bus, so we can walk to Berchtesgaden and visit the town, also have a drink. :)

Day 6 - Obersee

View trail on mapy.cz

We have the last day and night in the camp, so we decided to enjoy it and relax with a ferry trip and a short walk. It is a beautiful August day and there are some confusion in the harbor. Connections are strengthened and we are leaving in time. We decided to visit the opposite side of Königssee lake - the Salet harbor. The journey lasted for about an hour. We get off and head towards Lake Obersee. Approximately 20 minutes later, we come to the north side of the lake. Turned right and, with a slight climb, bypass the Obersee lake. The trail is rather narrow and slippery, but it is equipped with chains, so it is quite easy to get through. We even met some baby strollers (craziness). Due to the increased number of tourists, we arrive in about half an hour. We're relaxing, some of us bathing in really cold water. The way back is the same. If you are interested, you can get off at the St. Bartholomä and then continue the ride with next ferry. We did not take this opportunity, because there were already about million people in line waiting for the ferry.
